Mission statement

Tiffany & Co.'s mission is to enrich the lives of their customers by crafting timeless and exquisitely beautiful creations that hold deep sentimental value, destined to become cherished heirlooms passed down through generations. Their commitment to exceptional craftsmanship and enduring elegance ensures that each piece of jewelry represents not only a moment but a lasting legacy of love, style, and sophistication.

02


Design philosophy

Tiffany & Co.'s design philosophy centers on the belief that true beauty lies in simplicity, timelessness, and the finest quality materials. Their designs are characterized by clean lines, classic elegance, and a dedication to showcasing the inherent beauty of the precious materials they use. Tiffany's commitment to innovation and a respect for tradition results in pieces that transcend fleeting trends, making them not just stylish adornments but also cherished symbols of life's most meaningful moments, exemplifying a legacy of enduring style and sophistication.

03


Background history

Tiffany & Co., founded in 1837 by Charles Lewis Tiffany and John B. Young in New York City, initially operated as a "stationery and fancy goods emporium" before shifting its focus to jewelry in 1853. The brand revolutionized the jewelry industry with the introduction of the iconic Tiffany Setting ring, featuring six prongs to accentuate the diamond, and by marking prices on goods to eliminate haggling. Their commitment to uncompromising quality, unique design, and impeccable craftsmanship has made Tiffany a symbol of luxury and enduring elegance. Over the years, Tiffany has expanded its product line and presence globally, with a significant impact on popular culture.
