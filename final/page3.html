Style

Tiffany & Co.'s style has been significantly influenced by the visionary design leadership of Louis Comfort Tiffany, the son of co-founder Charles Lewis Tiffany. Louis Comfort Tiffany, who took the artistic reins in the early 20th century, introduced a fresh and distinctive design aesthetic characterized by Art Nouveau and Art Deco elements. His use of vibrant colors and innovative techniques in stained glass and decorative arts left an indelible mark on the brand, blending elegance with artistic ingenuity. This fusion of traditional craftsmanship and artistic innovation became a hallmark of Tiffany's design philosophy, shaping their unique, timeless style that continues to define the brand to this day.

05


Typography

Tiffany & Co.'s typography choices reflect their commitment to classic elegance and timeless design. The company's iconic logo prominently features the use of Sterling font, a clean and refined typeface that exudes sophistication. This font choice underscores the brand's heritage and longstanding tradition of luxury. Complementing Sterling font is the supporting type Santral, which provides a balanced and modern contrast, ensuring that their messaging and branding remain clear, legible, and contemporary while preserving the brand's timeless appeal.
